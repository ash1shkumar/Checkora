Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 2026

1. Information We Collect

Checkora is an open-source hybrid chess platform designed for transparent, secure, and performant online gameplay. We only collect basic information necessary to manage user authentication and orchestrate your chess matches:

  • Account Data: Username, email address, and encrypted password profiles when you choose to register.
  • Gameplay Metrics: Move history, win/loss stats, match durations, and bullet/blitz time preferences to compute accurate leaderboard rankings.
  • Technical Logs: Minimal background telemetry data such as session tokens to keep you securely logged in across page reloads.

2. How Your Data is Used

Your data remains dedicated exclusively to powering your active application experiences. We use the tracked parameters to:

  • Maintain your personalized player profile dashboard and performance metrics.
  • Calculate real-time material scores and process live move-validation checks.
  • Optimize the sync pipeline between our Python application handling state logic and the native C++ minimax computation core.

3. Cookies and Session Storage

We use essential cookies and local storage tokens to manage system authentication states and secure form submissions against Cross-Site Request Forgery (CSRF). No tracking cookies or behavioral profile monitors are embedded inside the platform infrastructure.

4. Data Retention & Safety Security

We retain your profile credentials and match logs for as long as your application account is kept active. Passwords are saved utilizing secure cryptographic hashing strategies built directly into Django's authentication modules. Because computations occur locally or across our dedicated architecture endpoints, your operational data is completely insulated from third-party advertising brokers.

5. Open Source Transparency & User Rights

In alignment with our open-source framework and GSSoC community policies, you maintain full authority over your data. You may request account closure or data wiping at any cycle. The community is welcome to inspect, audit, or review how data parsing is coded by inspecting our core architecture lines within our public GitHub Organization repository.

6. Contact & Community Channels

If you have any questions or feedback regarding this Privacy Policy, please open an issue thread on our GitHub issue board or contact the project maintainers directly via the Discord Community link.
